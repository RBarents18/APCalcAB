📚 Curriculum Map

AP Calculus AB Units

All 8 units aligned to the official AP Calculus AB framework—objectives, key skills, and AP Exam focus.

💡 Each unit folder in curriculum/ contains overview.md, objectives.md, skills.md, and practice.md. Use the GitHub links to navigate directly to source files.
1

Limits and Continuity

Students explore how limits allow us to understand function behavior near a point and at infinity, building the conceptual foundation for all of calculus.

  • Estimate limit values from graphs, tables & equations
  • Evaluate one-sided and infinite limits
  • Determine continuity at a point and over an interval
  • Classify discontinuities (removable, jump, essential)
  • Apply the Intermediate Value Theorem

2

Differentiation: Definition & Fundamental Properties

Students apply limits to define the derivative and develop fluency with basic differentiation rules across multiple representations.

  • Define and interpret the derivative as a rate of change
  • Apply power, product, and quotient rules
  • Differentiate trigonometric, exponential & logarithmic functions
  • Determine differentiability and connect to continuity
  • Write equations of tangent lines
Core View Files ↗
3

Differentiation: Composite, Implicit & Inverse Functions

Students master the chain rule, implicit differentiation, and derivatives of inverse functions, including higher-order derivatives.

  • Apply the chain rule to composite functions
  • Use implicit differentiation
  • Find derivatives of inverse and inverse trig functions
  • Compute higher-order derivatives
  • Justify derivative procedures symbolically
Core View Files ↗
4

Contextual Applications of Differentiation

Students apply derivatives to model and solve real-world problems involving rates of change, motion, and approximation.

  • Interpret derivatives in applied contexts
  • Model velocity and acceleration of a particle
  • Set up and solve related rates problems
  • Use linearization to approximate values
  • Apply L'Hôpital's Rule for indeterminate forms
Core View Files ↗
5

Analytical Applications of Differentiation

Students analyze relationships among a function and its derivatives to solve optimization problems and sketch graphs with mathematical justification.

  • Apply the Mean Value Theorem
  • Determine intervals of increase/decrease and critical points
  • Use first and second derivative tests
  • Determine concavity and locate inflection points
  • Solve applied optimization problems
Core View Files ↗
6

Integration and Accumulation of Change

Students apply limits to define definite integrals and discover how the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus connects differentiation and integration.

  • Compute and interpret Riemann sums
  • Evaluate definite integrals
  • Apply both parts of the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus
  • Find antiderivatives using u-substitution
  • Interpret definite integrals in applied contexts
Core View Files ↗
7

Differential Equations

Students solve differential equations and apply them to model exponential growth, decay, and other real-world phenomena.

  • Interpret and sketch slope fields
  • Approximate solutions using Euler's method
  • Solve separable differential equations
  • Model exponential growth and decay
  • Interpret solutions in applied contexts
Important View Files ↗
8

Applications of Integration

Students use integration to solve problems involving net change, area between curves, and volumes of 3-D solids.

  • Find the average value of a function
  • Analyze particle motion using integrals
  • Calculate area between curves
  • Find volume using disk and washer methods
  • Find volume using known cross-section shapes
