# Unit 2: Differentiation: Definition and Fundamental Properties — Learning Objectives

By the end of this unit, students will be able to:

1. Calculate average rates of change and explain their relationship to secant lines.
2. Write and evaluate the limit definition of the derivative.
3. Estimate the value of a derivative from a table or graph.
4. Determine where a function is differentiable and where it is not.
5. Explain the relationship between differentiability and continuity.
6. Apply the power rule to differentiate polynomial functions.
7. Apply the constant multiple rule and sum/difference rule.
8. Differentiate sin(x), cos(x), eˣ, and ln(x).
9. Apply the product rule to differentiate products of functions.
10. Apply the quotient rule to differentiate rational expressions.
11. Interpret the derivative as an instantaneous rate of change in context.
12. Write equations of tangent lines at a given point.
