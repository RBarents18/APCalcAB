# Unit 2: Differentiation: Definition and Fundamental Properties — Practice

## Starter Practice Problems

### Average Rate of Change
1. Find the average rate of change of f(x) = x² + 3x on [1, 4].
2. A particle moves along a line with position s(t) = t³ - 2t. Find the average velocity on [0, 3].

### Limit Definition of the Derivative
3. Use the limit definition to find f′(x) for f(x) = 3x² - 5.
4. Use the limit definition to find f′(2) for f(x) = x³.

### Basic Differentiation Rules
5. Differentiate: f(x) = 4x⁵ - 3x² + 7x - 2
6. Differentiate: g(x) = 6√x + 2/x
7. Find dy/dx for y = eˣ + sin(x) - ln(x)

### Product and Quotient Rules
8. Differentiate: f(x) = (2x + 1)(x³ - 4)
9. Differentiate: g(x) = (x² + 5) / (3x - 2)
10. Differentiate: h(x) = x² · eˣ

### Tangent Lines
11. Find the equation of the tangent line to f(x) = x³ - 2x at x = 1.
12. At what x-values does f(x) = x³ - 3x have a horizontal tangent line?

### Differentiability
13. Is f(x) = |x - 3| differentiable at x = 3? Explain using limits.
14. For a piecewise function, determine differentiability at the boundary point.

## Activity Ideas
- Derivative card sort: match functions to their derivatives
- Graphical analysis: sketch f′ from the graph of f
- Real-world rates: interpret derivative in position, velocity, temperature, or cost contexts
- Error analysis: identify and correct common product/quotient rule mistakes
