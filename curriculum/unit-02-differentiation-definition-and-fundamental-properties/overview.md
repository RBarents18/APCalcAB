# Unit 2: Differentiation: Definition and Fundamental Properties

## Description
Students apply limits to define the derivative and develop fluency with fundamental differentiation rules. Derivatives are interpreted as instantaneous rates of change and slopes of tangent lines.

## Major Topics
- Average rate of change and secant lines
- Definition of the derivative (limit of the difference quotient)
- Estimating derivatives from graphs and tables
- Differentiability and its relationship to continuity
- Basic differentiation rules (power, constant, sum/difference)
- Derivatives of sine, cosine, exponential (eˣ), and natural logarithm (ln x)
- Product rule and quotient rule

## AP Exam Relevance
Derivative fluency is essential for both MCQ and FRQ sections. Students must compute derivatives efficiently and interpret them in context.

## Evidence of Mastery
Students can:
- Define and compute derivatives using the limit definition
- Apply power, product, and quotient rules correctly
- Differentiate trigonometric, exponential, and logarithmic functions
- Explain when a function is not differentiable (corner, cusp, vertical tangent, discontinuity)
- Interpret derivatives as rates of change in applied contexts
