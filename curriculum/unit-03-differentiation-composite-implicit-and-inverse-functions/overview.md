# Unit 3: Differentiation: Composite, Implicit, and Inverse Functions

## Description
Students extend their differentiation skills to composite functions (chain rule), implicitly defined functions, and inverse functions including inverse trigonometric functions.

## Major Topics
- The chain rule
- Implicit differentiation
- Differentiating inverse functions
- Derivatives of inverse trigonometric functions
- Higher-order derivatives

## AP Exam Relevance
Chain rule and implicit differentiation appear frequently in both MCQ and FRQ sections, often combined with other derivative rules.

## Evidence of Mastery
Students can:
- Apply the chain rule to composite functions accurately
- Use implicit differentiation to find dy/dx for implicitly defined relations
- Find derivatives of inverse functions using the inverse function derivative formula
- Differentiate arcsin(x), arccos(x), and arctan(x)
- Compute second and higher-order derivatives
- Combine multiple differentiation rules in a single problem
