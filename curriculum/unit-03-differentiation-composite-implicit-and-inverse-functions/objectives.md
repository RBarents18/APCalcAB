# Unit 3: Differentiation: Composite, Implicit, and Inverse Functions — Learning Objectives

By the end of this unit, students will be able to:

1. Identify a composite function and its inner and outer components.
2. Apply the chain rule to differentiate composite functions.
3. Combine the chain rule with the product and quotient rules.
4. Differentiate implicitly defined relations to find dy/dx.
5. Use implicit differentiation to find the slope of a tangent line to a curve.
6. Apply the formula for the derivative of an inverse function.
7. Differentiate arcsin(x), arccos(x), and arctan(x).
8. Compute second derivatives using implicit differentiation.
9. Calculate higher-order derivatives (second, third) of explicit functions.
10. Interpret higher-order derivatives in motion and concavity contexts.
