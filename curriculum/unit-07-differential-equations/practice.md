# Unit 7: Differential Equations — Practice

## Starter Practice Problems

### Verifying Solutions
1. Verify that y = 3e^(2x) is a solution to dy/dx = 2y.
2. Verify that y = x² + C is a general solution to dy/dx = 2x.

### Slope Fields
3. Sketch a slope field for dy/dx = x + y using a 3×3 grid of points.
4. Given a slope field for dy/dx = y - x, sketch the particular solution through (0, 1).
5. Match each differential equation to its slope field (classroom activity).

### Euler's Method
6. Use Euler's method with step size Δx = 0.5 to approximate y(2) for dy/dx = x - y with y(0) = 1. Show all steps in a table.
7. Explain how decreasing the step size affects the accuracy of Euler's method.

### Separation of Variables
8. Solve: dy/dx = 2xy, y(0) = 3
9. Solve: dy/dx = (x²) / y, y(0) = 2
10. Solve: dy/dx = y·cos(x), y(0) = 1

### Exponential Models
11. A population of 500 bacteria grows at a rate proportional to its size. After 3 hours, there are 2000 bacteria. Write a model P(t) and find P(5).
12. A radioactive substance has a half-life of 10 years. Write a decay model and find how long until 25% remains.

### Logistic Growth (Conceptual)
13. A population follows logistic growth with carrying capacity L = 1000. Describe:
    - What happens when the population is much less than L?
    - What happens when the population is close to L?
    - Where is the growth rate fastest?

## Activity Ideas
- Slope field card match: match DEs to their slope field sketches
- Euler's method spreadsheet: build an approximation table in a shared Google Sheet
- Exponential model real data: use population or temperature data to find k
- AP FRQ slope field: interpret a slope field, sketch a solution curve, solve the DE
