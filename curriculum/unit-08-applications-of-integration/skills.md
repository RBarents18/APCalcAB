# Unit 8: Applications of Integration — Skills

## AP Calculus AB Skill Categories

### Skill 1: Implementing Mathematical Processes
- Calculate average value: (1/(b-a)) · ∫(a to b) f(x)dx
- Set up area between curves: ∫(a to b) [f(x) - g(x)]dx where f(x) ≥ g(x)
- Find intersection points to determine limits of integration
- Set up cross-sectional volume: V = ∫(a to b) A(x)dx where A(x) is the area of a cross section
- Set up disk method: V = π∫(a to b) [R(x)]²dx
- Set up washer method: V = π∫(a to b) ([R(x)]² - [r(x)]²)dx

### Skill 2: Connecting Representations
- Interpret the average value as a height that gives the same area as the original function
- Connect net displacement vs. total distance to signed vs. unsigned area
- Visualize the solid of revolution being generated by rotating a region

### Skill 3: Justification
- Justify limits of integration using intersection points or problem constraints
- Explain the geometric meaning of the integrand in area and volume problems
- Justify why the washer method requires subtracting the inner radius squared

### Skill 4: Communication and Notation
- Set up integrals fully before evaluating (show the setup)
- Label the axis of rotation and the outer/inner radii in volume problems
- Express average value, area, and volume answers with appropriate units

## Key Vocabulary
- Average value, Mean Value Theorem for Integrals
- Net displacement, total distance traveled
- Area between curves, bounded region
- Volume of a solid, cross section, disk method, washer method
- Axis of rotation, outer radius, inner radius, solid of revolution
