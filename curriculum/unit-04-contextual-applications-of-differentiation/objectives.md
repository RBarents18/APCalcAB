# Unit 4: Contextual Applications of Differentiation — Learning Objectives

By the end of this unit, students will be able to:

1. Interpret the sign and value of a derivative in a real-world situation.
2. Identify units of a derivative given the units of input and output.
3. Define position, velocity, and acceleration and describe their relationships.
4. Determine when a particle is moving right, left, or at rest.
5. Find total distance traveled versus net displacement for a particle.
6. Set up related rates problems by identifying a geometric or applied relationship.
7. Differentiate a related rates equation implicitly with respect to time.
8. Solve for an unknown rate given known rates and values.
9. Write a linearization (tangent line approximation) for a function at a point.
10. Use a linearization to estimate a function value and assess its accuracy.
11. Identify indeterminate forms and apply L'Hôpital's Rule appropriately.
12. Recognize when L'Hôpital's Rule does not apply.
