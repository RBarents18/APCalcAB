# Unit 4: Contextual Applications of Differentiation — Practice

## Starter Practice Problems

### Interpreting Derivatives in Context
1. The temperature of a cup of coffee at time t minutes is T(t) degrees Fahrenheit. Interpret T′(5) = -3 in context, including units.
2. A company's revenue is R(x) dollars when x units are sold. If R′(200) = 12, what does this tell you?

### Particle Motion
3. A particle's position is s(t) = t³ - 6t² + 9t for t ≥ 0 (in seconds, feet).
   - Find velocity v(t) and acceleration a(t).
   - When is the particle moving right? Moving left? At rest?
   - Find the total distance traveled on [0, 5].

### Related Rates
4. A ladder 10 ft long rests against a vertical wall. If the bottom slides away at 2 ft/sec, how fast is the top sliding down when the bottom is 6 ft from the wall?
5. A spherical balloon is inflated so that its volume increases at 100 cm³/sec. How fast is the radius increasing when r = 5 cm?
6. Sand pours into a conical pile at 12 ft³/min. The height always equals the diameter. How fast is the height growing when h = 4 ft?

### Linearization
7. Find the linearization of f(x) = √x at x = 9. Use it to approximate √9.2.
8. Use a linearization of sin(x) at x = 0 to estimate sin(0.1).

### L'Hôpital's Rule
9. Evaluate: lim(x→0) sin(3x) / (5x)
10. Evaluate: lim(x→∞) (2x² + 3x) / (x² - 1)

## Activity Ideas
- Related rates real-world workshop: choose a scenario (ladder, water tank, shadow)
- Particle motion graphing: sketch v(t) and a(t) given s(t)
- AP FRQ practice: full related rates problem with complete write-up
- Linearization exploration: compare approximation accuracy at different x values
