# Unit 4: Contextual Applications of Differentiation

## Description
Students apply derivatives to model and solve real-world problems involving rates of change, motion, related rates, and local linear approximation.

## Major Topics
- Interpreting the meaning of derivatives in context
- Straight-line motion: position, velocity, acceleration
- Related rates
- Linearization (local linear approximation)
- L'Hôpital's Rule for indeterminate forms

## AP Exam Relevance
Contextual applications—especially related rates and particle motion—appear regularly in AP FRQ sections. Clear communication of meaning is essential.

## Evidence of Mastery
Students can:
- Interpret the value and sign of a derivative in a real-world context
- Solve related rates problems by setting up and differentiating an equation with respect to time
- Write and use a linear approximation to estimate function values
- Apply L'Hôpital's Rule to evaluate limits of indeterminate forms (0/0 and ∞/∞)
- Analyze particle motion using position, velocity, and acceleration functions
