# Unit 6: Integration and Accumulation of Change

## Description
Students use limits to define definite integrals as accumulation of change and explore the deep connection between differentiation and integration through the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus.

## Major Topics
- Introducing accumulation of change
- Riemann sums (left, right, midpoint, trapezoidal)
- Definite integrals and integral notation
- Accumulation functions and their properties
- Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (Parts 1 and 2)
- Antiderivatives and indefinite integrals
- Integrating using substitution (u-substitution)
- Properties of definite integrals

## AP Exam Relevance
Integration is central to the AP exam. FRQ problems frequently involve interpreting integrals in context, computing accumulated change, and applying the FTC.

## Evidence of Mastery
Students can:
- Estimate a definite integral using Riemann sums
- Evaluate definite integrals using antiderivatives
- Apply both parts of the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus
- Compute indefinite integrals using basic rules and u-substitution
- Interpret a definite integral as total accumulated change in context
