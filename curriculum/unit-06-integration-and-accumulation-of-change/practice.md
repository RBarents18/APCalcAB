# Unit 6: Integration and Accumulation of Change — Practice

## Starter Practice Problems

### Riemann Sums
1. Approximate ∫(0 to 4) f(x)dx using a left Riemann sum with 4 equal subintervals, given:

| x    | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 |
|------|---|---|---|---|---|
| f(x) | 3 | 5 | 2 | 7 | 4 |

2. Repeat problem 1 using a right Riemann sum and a trapezoidal approximation.
3. Explain which approximation is an overestimate if f is increasing on [0, 4].

### Definite Integrals
4. Evaluate: ∫(1 to 3) (2x + 5)dx
5. Evaluate: ∫(0 to π) sin(x)dx
6. Given ∫(0 to 5) f(x)dx = 12 and ∫(0 to 2) f(x)dx = 4, find ∫(2 to 5) f(x)dx.

### Fundamental Theorem of Calculus
7. Let F(x) = ∫(0 to x) (t² + 1)dt. Find F′(x). Evaluate F(3).
8. Find d/dx[∫(1 to x²) cos(t)dt].
9. Evaluate ∫(0 to 4) √x dx using FTC Part 2.

### Antiderivatives and u-Substitution
10. Find: ∫ (3x² - 4x + 7)dx
11. Find: ∫ sin(5x)dx using u-substitution
12. Evaluate: ∫(0 to 1) 2x·eˣ² dx
13. Find: ∫ x/(x² + 1)dx

### Interpretation
14. A car's velocity is v(t) = 4t - t² ft/sec. What is the net displacement from t = 0 to t = 4?
15. Rain falls at the rate r(t) = 0.5t inches/hour. How many total inches fall between t = 0 and t = 6?

## Activity Ideas
- Riemann sum visual: shade rectangles on a graph and compare sums
- FTC exploration: evaluate accumulation function values at different upper limits
- u-substitution scavenger hunt: match integrals to their substitution strategy
- AP FRQ practice: interpret a rate-of-change integral in a real context
