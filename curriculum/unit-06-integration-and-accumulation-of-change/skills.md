# Unit 6: Integration and Accumulation of Change — Skills

## AP Calculus AB Skill Categories

### Skill 1: Implementing Mathematical Processes
- Compute left, right, midpoint, and trapezoidal Riemann sums from a table or function
- Evaluate basic antiderivatives: power rule, trig functions, eˣ, 1/x
- Apply u-substitution to evaluate both indefinite and definite integrals
- Apply FTC Part 1: d/dx[∫(a to x) f(t)dt] = f(x)
- Apply FTC Part 2: ∫(a to b) f(x)dx = F(b) - F(a)

### Skill 2: Connecting Representations
- Relate the area under a rate-of-change curve to total accumulated change
- Connect a definite integral to the area between a curve and the x-axis
- Interpret accumulation functions graphically
- Estimate integrals using Riemann sums from a table or graph

### Skill 3: Justification
- Justify the use of FTC given conditions of continuity
- Explain the meaning of a definite integral in context with correct units
- Use net change theorem with a written interpretation

### Skill 4: Communication and Notation
- Write indefinite integrals with the constant of integration (+ C)
- Use integral notation precisely: ∫(a to b) f(x)dx
- Express the value of an integral in context with appropriate units

## Key Vocabulary
- Riemann sum, left/right/midpoint/trapezoidal approximation
- Definite integral, indefinite integral, antiderivative
- Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (FTC), accumulation function, net change
- u-substitution, change of variable
- Area under a curve, accumulated change
