# Unit 1: Limits and Continuity

## Description
Students explore how limits allow us to understand function behavior near a point and at infinity, building the conceptual foundation for all of calculus.

## Major Topics
- Introducing limits and estimating limit values
- Defining limits and using limit notation
- Estimating limit values from graphs and tables
- Algebraic properties of limits
- One-sided limits
- Infinite limits and limits at infinity
- Continuity at a point and over an interval
- Types of discontinuities (removable, jump, essential)
- Intermediate Value Theorem

## AP Exam Relevance
Limits and continuity appear throughout both multiple-choice and free-response sections. Students must move fluently among graphical, numerical, verbal, and symbolic representations.

## Evidence of Mastery
Students can:
- Evaluate limits numerically, graphically, and algebraically
- Determine whether a function is continuous at a point or on an interval
- Classify discontinuities by type and justify the classification
- Apply the Intermediate Value Theorem to make existence arguments
- Interpret limits and continuity in context
